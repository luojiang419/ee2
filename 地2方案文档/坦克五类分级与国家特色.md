# Epoch 11-12 Tank Classification

Classification rule for this file:
- Do **not** use current copied gameplay stats as the primary reference.
- Classify mainly by historical name, intended battlefield role, and a World of Tanks style role model.
- If a unit does not exist in Epoch 11, do not force it into Epoch 11.
- Epoch 11 mainly contains armored cars, light tanks, and main battle tanks.
- Epoch 12 starts introducing heavy tanks and tank destroyers more clearly.

## Class Definitions

1. Armored car
   - RPS: Light_Mounted
   - Fragile, fast, transports infantry, suppresses citizens and infantry with MG/autocannon fire.

2. Light tank
   - RPS: Heavy_Mounted
   - Mobile gun tank, counters armored cars, can fight infantry, may transport a small number of infantry.

3. Main battle tank
   - RPS: Heavy_Mounted
   - Balanced all-round tank.

4. Heavy tank
   - RPS: Heavy_Mounted
   - Slow frontline bruiser, soaks damage, pushes lines, destroys towers/buildings, good vs medium tanks, poor vs infantry and mobile targets.

5. Tank destroyer
   - RPS: Light_Artillery
   - Long-range anti-tank sniper, should not behave like a line tank.

## World of Tanks style parameter rules

For tanks that clearly exist in World of Tanks or have a very similar role there, use WoT-style role identity as the main reference for flavor tuning.

Do not copy current in-project stats when they conflict with intended identity. Instead, tune around these gameplay dimensions:

| Parameter | Meaning in EE2X tuning |
| --- | --- |
| Top speed | March speed / chase capability / repositioning |
| Hull traverse | Turn rate while moving; affects circling, close-range flexibility, and anti-flank response |
| Turret traverse | Gun tracking speed; for turretless TDs this should be very poor or absent |
| Reload | Sustained damage output; low reload = faster firing |
| Range | Effective anti-armor reach |
| Shell velocity | Hit consistency against moving targets; high velocity favors sniping and anti-armor identity |
| Alpha damage | Single-shot punishment; high alpha fits heavies and TDs more than light vehicles |
| Accuracy / dispersion feel | Can be represented indirectly with projectile speed, reload, traverse, and minimum range behavior |
| Durability | HP and staying power on the front line |
| Anti-infantry ability | Splash, MG behavior, fire rate, and turret responsiveness |

### Core class targets

| Class | Speed | Traverse | Reload | Range | Shell velocity | Durability | Anti-infantry |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Armored car | Very high | Very high | Fast | Short-Medium | Medium | Low | Strong vs citizens/light infantry |
| Light tank | High | High | Fast-Medium | Medium | Medium-High | Low-Medium | Good |
| Main battle tank | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium | High | Medium-High | Medium |
| Heavy tank | Low | Low | Slow | Medium | Medium | Very high | Weak-Medium |
| Tank destroyer | Low-Medium | Very low if casemate | Slow | Long-Very long | Very high | Medium-High | Weak |

### Translation rules for EE2X

- Armored cars should feel dangerous to unarmored targets, but should lose direct fights against real tanks.
- Light tanks should beat armored cars through gun quality and mobility, not raw armor.
- Main battle tanks should be the most stable all-round choice.
- Heavy tanks should win frontal trades, but suffer badly when swarmed by infantry or flanked.
- Tank destroyers should win by distance, not by brawling.
- If a vehicle is turretless in reality, reduce traverse response and make flanking matter.
- If a vehicle is known for strong gun handling or stabilizer quality, keep aim responsiveness and shell velocity high.
- If a vehicle is known for poor armor but high mobility, do not compensate by giving it universal power.

## Per-nation detailed tuning notes

### American
- Armored cars: good transport utility, smooth handling, decent MG suppression.
- Light tanks: flexible, comfortable, not extreme in armor.
- MBTs: balanced, dependable, good shell velocity and reasonable turn response.
- TDs: strong mobility and good anti-tank gun feel; `M18Hellcat` should be one of the fastest TD-style vehicles.
- Design note: American tanks should rarely be the absolute best at one stat, but should feel easy to use.

### Russian
- Armored cars: serviceable, but less refined than Western wheeled vehicles.
- Light tanks: direct and aggressive, favor pressure over finesse.
- MBTs: good frontal fighting and punch, but less precision comfort.
- Heavies: major national strength; slow, punishing, durable, high-alpha bias.
- TDs: strong anti-tank strike, less traverse comfort, less flexible than American or German TDs.
- Design note: Russian line should reward direct engagement and punish overexposing flanks less than precision mistakes.

### Chinese
- Armored cars: fast and practical, not as refined as the best Western scout cars.
- Light tanks: mobile pressure tanks.
- MBTs: modern aggressive line with good mobility and strong gun threat.
- Design note: Chinese modern tanks should feel like offensive MBTs rather than pure snipers.

### MiddleEast
- MBTs: emphasize protection, battlefield endurance, and stable frontline value.
- Design note: fewer vehicle types in the current list means the Merkava-style identity should be especially clear.

### Roman
- Armored cars: mobility first.
- Light tanks / MBTs: competent gun platforms, decent mobility, not top-tier armor monsters.
- TD/assault-gun branch: good opportunity for strong gun / lower armor identity.
- Design note: Roman line can lean into flexible maneuver warfare rather than pure durability.

### German
- Armored cars: competent but not the main national fantasy.
- Light tanks: efficient, accurate, good at using gun quality.
- MBTs: precise, high shell velocity, strong medium-long range performance.
- Heavies: methodical, slower, high penetration feel.
- TDs: core national strength; `Hetzer`, `elefant`, `Jagdtiger` should have especially strong anti-tank identity and weaker anti-infantry flexibility.
- Design note: Germany should be the easiest nation to make feel like a true sniper / precision armor line.

### English
- Armored cars: practical but not dominant in pure speed races.
- Light tanks: transitional and utility-focused.
- MBTs: armored and composed, not hyper-mobile.
- Heavies: slow, tough, line-holding vehicles; Churchill-family should embody this clearly.
- TDs: high-range, disciplined anti-tank role; `Archer` should feel awkward in close combat but deadly at distance.
- Design note: British line should reward position and frontal fights more than chase potential.

### French
- Armored cars: fast and active.
- Light tanks: mobility-focused, lower staying power.
- MBTs: faster and more fluid than heavy line tanks from other nations.
- Heavies: exceptions exist, but should not erase the nation’s mobility identity.
- TDs: mobile, opportunistic, less bunker-like than German TDs.
- Design note: France should feel fast and skill-expressive, not armor-dominant.

### Japanese
- Armored cars / light tanks: lighter and more fragile in earlier-era entries.
- MBTs: modern vehicles should be responsive, fairly quick, and gun-reliable.
- Heavy branch: if kept, should be slower and more niche than the modern MBT branch.
- Design note: do not let old Japanese light vehicles become fake heavies through copied stats.

### Greek
- Use a balanced export-tank interpretation unless a stronger regional identity is added later.
- PT91 can lean slightly more mobile/aggressive than a classic heavy line.
- Design note: keep it coherent first; give unique flavor later.

### Korean
- Light tanks: mobile and responsive.
- MBTs: fast modern MBTs with good acceleration feel, decent reload, and reliable shell velocity.
- Design note: Korea should feel like a modern high-readiness armored force.

### Mayan
- Use the base vehicle’s real-world role first.
- Only add regional uniqueness after the class role is functioning well.
- Design note: avoid using regional flavor as an excuse to blur core tank class identity.

## Practical implementation guidance

When later tuning a specific vehicle, follow this order:
1. Confirm the vehicle class from the classification list above.
2. Apply the nation’s flavor from the national table.
3. If the tank exists in WoT, use WoT-style identity as the main inspiration for:
   - top speed
   - reload rhythm
   - effective fighting range
   - hull/turret turning feel
   - shell velocity
4. Only after role identity is clear, adjust cost, HP, and transport utility.
5. Avoid making one vehicle good at everything. Every class should keep real weaknesses.

## Important constraint

If a vehicle clearly belongs to a role in historical usage or WoT identity, do not let copied legacy data override that role. Rebalance the gameplay to match the intended identity instead of preserving bad inherited numbers.
